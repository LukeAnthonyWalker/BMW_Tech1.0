i PERFORMANCE

Zero Emissions

In the BMW iPerformance models, an efficient combustion engine works in perfect harmony with a powerful electric motor. The conventional drive offers the range while the electric drive scores particularly highly in urban traffic, thanks to its zero emissions and almost silent driving dynamics.

Reliability

BMW's iPerformance package allows for realiable cars to be manufactured, this is because of the lack of moving parts in the cars. It's a technical, yet simple system used to power the cars, but has been thouroughly tested.

Electric!

The BMW iPerformance vehicles use the BMW eDrive electric drive technology developed by BMW i. They are able to cover short and medium distances purely electrically with the innovative combination of electric motor, lithium-ion battery and intelligent energy management.

Government Grant

You can get a grant towards the cost of a new electric (plug-in) car, van or motorcycle as long as it meets certain conditions. The ‘cost’ is the full purchase price you pay for the basic vehicle - including number plates, vehicle excise duty and VAT. It doesn’t include delivery charges, the first registration fee or any optional extras.

BMW i Performance

Experience has always been a key factor in implementing innovative automotive concepts. And in the BMW plug-in hybrids, this influence has been particularly valuable. Their drive system consists of an efficient combustion engine and a powerful electric motor. This is how they combine the typical BMW qualities with the advantages of electric driving and thus profit directly from the expertise of the BMW i engineers to such an extent that they deserve their own designation: BMW iPerformance Automobiles.

Why is the i Package so expensive?

For an electric vehicle of similar size, you need about a 26 kilowatt-hour lithium ion battery pack for a 100-mile nominal range. Manufacturers are cagey about hard numbers, but current estimates of battery costs range anywhere from about $650 to $1,200 per kilowatt-hour. That would put the battery cost at anywhere from $16,000 to over $30,000.
